Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our therapy services and approach.

Behavior therapy is a form of psychological treatment that focuses on identifying and changing problematic behaviors and thought patterns. It's based on the principle that our behaviors, thoughts, and feelings are interconnected. By changing negative behaviors and thought patterns, we can improve emotional well-being and daily functioning. Our therapists use evidence-based techniques such as Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), exposure therapy, and behavioral activation to help clients develop healthier coping mechanisms and achieve their therapeutic goals.

The timeline for seeing results varies depending on individual circumstances, the nature of the challenges being addressed, and your commitment to the therapeutic process. Many clients report noticing positive changes within the first few weeks of therapy, such as improved mood or reduced anxiety. More significant behavioral and thought pattern changes typically emerge over several months of consistent work. We establish clear goals during your initial assessment and monitor progress regularly, adjusting your treatment plan as needed to ensure you're on track toward your objectives.

Yes, therapy is confidential. All information shared during sessions is protected by therapist-client privilege and cannot be disclosed without your written consent. However, there are important legal exceptions where we are required to break confidentiality: when there is imminent danger to yourself or others, suspected child or elder abuse, or when a court orders disclosure. We will always inform you if we need to break confidentiality and explain the circumstances. Your privacy and trust are paramount to us, and we maintain strict confidentiality standards in all aspects of our practice.
